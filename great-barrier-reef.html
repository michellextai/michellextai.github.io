Great Barrier Reef

Yiran Buckley, Tony Deng, Rob Feeney, and I teamed up to create a non-interactive dashboard experience–with environmental activists’ interests in mind–that incentivizes policy makers to take action in protecting Australia’s Great Barrier Reef.

Research and Sketches

We started the project by researching the biodiversity in Australia and sorting what is considered most important for environmental activists, policy makers, and Australia’s residents. The research phase was an important step in developing a understanding that the project is a success only if we have the buy in from both environmental activists and policy makers. After extensive research on temperature changes, climate, species, habitat loss, and the Great Barrier Reef in Australia, we each drafted sketches on how we may visualize changes in biodiversity.



Design Process

I envisioned a cohesive storyline to the dashboard to help policy makers decipher the raw data, so I iterated through each piece with hierarchy in mind. I also understood that for a non-interactive dashboard to be convincing and engaging, drastic motion graphics must only be used to draw attention in critical moments.
