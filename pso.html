    Project Scope

    In the 2014-2015 season, Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra saw a gap between its strategic planning and its audience. Revenues dipped. After raising ticket prices for two consecutive seasons, the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra has seen its 2014-2015 sales for Mellon Grand Classics drop 11 percent. The Pops series dropped from 6 percent. While revenue per seat sold went up, overall ticket revenue was down 4 percent for 2014-2015. Orchestras face many challenges in trying to fill their halls, and most orchestras in cities of comparable population play in smaller halls. Pittsburgh Symphony faces filling a particularly large hall, one with 2,662 seats for sale. Marketing research can address Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra challenges, specifically the changing buying patterns in full-season subscriptions, the stagnant nonsubscriber ticket sales, and addressing empty seats in the concert hall. From learning current orchestral patterns and customer perspectives through marketing research, Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra can effectively make changes to their system in order to increase concert-goers and revenue.
