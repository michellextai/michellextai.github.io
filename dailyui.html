Daily UI

Following Chimdindu Aneke’s take on the Daily UI challenge, I was inspired to create meaningful interactions every day and to explore new design methods and tools. This is an ongoing project, where I frame each day’s challenge around a new space that may benefit from a redesign. I then take the reframed challenge and treat it as a whiteboard challenge, mapping out user needs, user goals, and potential task flows before sketching and digitizing the design ideas.

Day 001 Sign Up

I started this project as motivation to start a full-length project. To be named Vault, this project is a passion project I started to help people build their productivity practice through a flexible medium for documentation of tasks, events, and notes. Look forward to the complete project and the process I took! For now, this is the signup page that I created for Vault, with motion graphics created in Origami Studio.

Day 002 Checkout

For this prompt, I wanted to see if there were any areas I may make an impact. In the spirit of recent calls for donations and help for Hurrican Harvey, I looked at long-term causes that every person may benefit. I chose to reimagine the donations checkout process for Khan Academy on the mobile phone, whose mission is to "provide a free, world-class education to anyone, anywhere." I kept the brand identity of Khan Academy through my design process and created the motion graphics in Origami Studio.




Delight with visual cues

The interactive slider gives users more power selecting how much money they would like to contribute than a tiled option allows. Users are given instant feedback with the visualization of the rising stack of money and the real-time changes in the amount of money directly above the slider, bringing delight to the interaction.

Flexible with quick interactions

The quick keyboard input to select a dollar amount gives users a quick way to continue on their donations process. Changes are instant and no-nonsense, respecting users’ time with competing priorities.
